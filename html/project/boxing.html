The Best Boxers in History

Author: Casey Vang

What even is Boxing?

Boxing is a combat sport and martial art where two opponents face each other in a ring to compete using only their fists. Each fighter wears padded gloves and attempts to land punches on their opponent while avoiding being hit. The sport is governed by rules that emphasize safety, fairness, and skill.

What makes a good Boxer?

There are various factors that make up a good boxer such as...

  • Footwork: A boxers ability to move and stay light on their feet is vital for evading and manuerving around their opponent.
  • Cardio & Endurance: Having great stamina will allow you to last longer in rounds, and not get torn apart by your opponent.
  • Startegy and Ring IQ: Being able to seek every oppurtinty to strike and create angles, whilst defending and countering against your opponent, will vastly bring you higher than most boxers

Top 5 Boxers:

  1. Mike Tyson:

    Mike Tyson, born Michael Gerard Tyson on June 30, 1966, in Brooklyn, New York, is a legendary American boxer widely regarded as one of the most ferocious and dominant heavyweight champions in boxing history. Known as "Iron Mike" and "The Baddest Man on the Planet," Tyson captivated the boxing world with his extraordinary power, speed, and intimidating presence.

  2. Muhammad Ali

    Known as "The Greatest," Ali's unmatched combination of speed, skill, and charisma made him an icon. A three-time heavyweight champion with legendary bouts against Joe Frazier, George Foreman, and Sonny Liston.
